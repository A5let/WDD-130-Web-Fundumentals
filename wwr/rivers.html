Snake River, Wyoming

The Snake River in Wyoming offers an unforgettable adventure through the heart of Grand Teton National Park. This scenic river provides class II-III rapids perfect for both beginners and intermediate rafters. The journey takes you beneath towering peaks, through pristine forests, and past abundant wildlife including elk, moose, and bald eagles.

Our expert guides navigate the famous rapids like Big Kahuna and Lunch Counter while sharing fascinating details about the area's geology and history. Each bend in the river reveals new breathtaking views of the Teton Range.

  • Group Size: 4-12 people
  • Cost: $95 per person (half-day), $165 (full-day)
  • Season: May through September
  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • Highlights: Wildlife viewing, mountain vistas, family-friendly rapids

Salmon River, Idaho

Known as the "River of No Return," Idaho's Salmon River runs through the largest wilderness area in the lower 48 states. This legendary waterway offers both serene stretches and heart-pounding rapids, making it perfect for multi-day adventures. The river cuts through a deep canyon that's deeper than the Grand Canyon in some places.

Our experienced guides lead expeditions through remote wilderness, where you'll camp on pristine beaches, soak in natural hot springs, and explore Native American pictographs. The crystal-clear waters teem with salmon, and the surrounding hills are home to bighorn sheep and black bears.

  • Group Size: 6-15 people
  • Cost: $225 per person (day trip), $1,900 (5-day expedition)
  • Season: June through September
  • Difficulty: Class III-IV rapids
  • Highlights: Hot springs, wilderness camping, historical sites

Gallatin River, Montana

The Gallatin River, immortalized in the film "A River Runs Through It," winds through the pristine wilderness of southwestern Montana. Starting in Yellowstone National Park, this river offers diverse rafting experiences from gentle float trips to challenging whitewater adventures. The Mad Mile section provides an exhilarating series of continuous rapids that will test your rafting skills.

Our trips showcase Montana's stunning landscape, including towering canyon walls, lush forests, and abundant wildlife. Experienced guides share their knowledge of local ecology and the river's role in the region's development. The Gallatin is also renowned for its world-class fly fishing opportunities.

  • Group Size: 2-8 people
  • Cost: $85 per person (half-day), $145 (full-day)
  • Season: Late May through August
  • Difficulty: Class I-IV rapids
  • Highlights: Scenic canyons, fly fishing, film location sites
